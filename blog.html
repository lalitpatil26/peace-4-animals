-Habitat protection: Our NGO work to protect natural habitats like national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and forest reserves. They may also buy land to create new protected areas.

-Anti-poaching: Our NGO work prevent poaching and wildlife trafficking through  patrols and other efforts. 

-Wildlife rescue and rehabilitation: Our NGO work to rescue and rehabilitate injured wildlife. 

-Education and awareness: Our NGO educate the public about the importance of biodiversity,sustainable resource management, and the consequences of wildlife trafficking. 

-Eco-tourism: NGOs work with local communities to create nature-based tourism experiences that support conservation efforts and provide sustainable livelihoods. 

-Legislation: NGOs work with governments to write legislation to protect species, like the Endangered Species Act (ESA) in the United States. 

-Combating diseases: NGOs use their health expertise to investigate and combat diseases that threaten the health of people, domestic animals, and wildlife. 

-Climate change: NGOs work to address climate change.Law enforcement: NGOs work with law enforcement to prosecute wildlifecrimes. 

- Monitoring and reporting: NGOs use tools like the Spatial Monitoring and Reporting Tool (SMART) to improve anti-poaching efforts.

-Citizen science: NGOs use citizen science projects to highlight issues,like the effects of linear infrastructures cutting across wildlife habitats.
